Staff Engineer is a well-structured guide for engineers in traditional, leveled tech organizations who are thinking about the staff track. If that's you, it delivers.

Larson's four archetypes (Tech Lead, Architect, Solver, Right Hand) are a useful framework, and the core insight that staff work is defined by scope and ambiguity rather than technical depth is worth internalizing. The Tech Lead archetype in particular feels the most universally recognizable, closest to what many senior engineers are already doing informally.

The interview sections, while grounding in theory, get repetitive in practice. Reading them back to back reveals common talking points and patterns, which somewhat undermines the "every company is different" framing the book leans on.

The main limitation is that the book works best if you're operating in a traditional, leveled org. In flatter or less hierarchical companies, some of the advice doesn't map cleanly, and there's less here about how to operate effectively as a senior engineer in general. It's a focused book, and that focus is both its strength and its constraint.

Still worth a read if you're on or approaching the staff track.
